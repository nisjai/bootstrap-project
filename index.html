Youth National Basketball Championship

HYDERABAD: A basketball feast is on the cards from Saturday when the 34th Youth National Basketball Championship for boys and girls kicks off at the GMC Balayogi Indoor Stadium, Gachibowli, on July 1. Fifty one teams from 26 states will vie for honours in the championship which will be played on the two wooden courts laid out at the Indoor stadium and a couple of matches will be held at the IIIT Hyderabad campus. A total of 85 league matches and 52 knock-out games will be held in the tournament, which will end on July 8. The matches will commence at 7 a.m. daily.

Raksha Bandhan Update

That is how Prime Minister celebrated Rakshabandhan

Prime Minister Narendra Modi on Monday greeted the nation on the occasion of Raksha Bandhan. “On the auspicious occasion of Raksha Bandhan, congratulations to the nation. Greetings on Raksha Bandhan,” he said on Twitter early in the day. Modi also celebrated the festival with the widows from Vrindavan who came to Delhi to tie hand-made rakhis on his wrist. The PM also met schoolchildren at his office to celebrate Raksha Bandhan.
